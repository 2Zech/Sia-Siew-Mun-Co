Sia Siew Mun & Co. was established in 2003 where her practice is focused on dispute resolution.


The firm prides herself in helping her clients appreciate the true nature and effect of the issues in hand and providing them with strategic and practical options.


The firm is helmed by a dedicated team of lawyers and supporting staff who work on every matter with a fervent desire for the best possible outcome.


People

Sia Siew Mun


Siew Mun is the founding and managing partner who has been in active practice since 1999. She was conferred the coveted Best Mooter Award when she was reading law in the University of Kent, at Canterbury (LLB Law with Accounting). Having obtained the Certificate of Legal Practice, she has been practising civil and commercial litigation exclusively since then. Siew Mun leads the team with her strategic thinking. Her legal submissions in court are distinctively concise and succinct.

Alvin Oh Seong Yew


Alvin is a partner who has been in active dispute-resolution practice since 2011. He read law at the University of Hull and was called to the English Bar as a Barrister by the Honourable Society of the Middle Temple in 2010. He was then admitted as an Advocate & Solicitor of the High Court of Malaya in 2011. Alvin is a keen trial lawyer and is adept at cross-examination of various types of witnesses. He has acted for clients in all manner of construction, commercial and corporate disputes and has appeared in all levels of Malaysian Courts. In construction disputes, Alvin has experience in acting for Developers in Court proceedings and arbitrations. He is also actively involved in Malaysian Bar activities and has been a serving committee member of the Kuala Lumpur Bar Committee since 2016.

Ng Kien Hann Tristan


Tristan is a partner of the firm and has been in practice since his admission as an Advocate and Solicitor in 2016. Tristan graduated with a Bachelors of Law from the University of Liverpool in 2013 and obtained his Certificate of Legal Practice in 2014. Tristan's practice is in dispute resolution and over his years of practice represented and advised clients in matters relating to Construction Law, Commercial Law, Corporate Disputes and Restructuring, Employment Law and Defamation. Tristan also has experience in adjudication claims filed under the Construction Industry Payment and Adjudication Act 2012.

Sew Chang Peng


Sew has been exclusively practising dispute-resolution since 2018. He graduated from University of London (External Programme) in 2016. He interned at the firm in 2016. As a graduate of the Certificate in Legal Practice, Sew was Siew Mun’s Pupil-in- chambers before being admitted as an Advocate & Solicitor of the High Court of Malaya in 2018. After a short stint to pursue his criminal law interests, he rejoined the firm as an associate. Sew has a developing practice in the firm’s core practice areas. Sew’s proficiency in reviewing documents and identifying the issues is vital to the team.

CONSTRUCTION




Our construction practice is mainly developer-based and we have acted for clients in instituting and defending claims brought via the Construction Industry Payment and Adjudication Act 2012, arbitration and Court proceedings.


We have advised on or acted for:


developer in challenging claims by contractors for extension of time as well as their entitlements to liquidated damages under various forms of construction contracts;


a major highway concessionaire in defending a claim that its excavations works have caused the collapse of a bridge where geotechnical expert evidence was involved;


prosecuted claims for negligent construction and/or defective design of a bridge;


defects on building structures caused by building not in accordance to drawings and specifications;


architects against homeowners’ claims for purported negligence in the discharge of their duties.
